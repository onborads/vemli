                        <h1>
                          How to Export Receipts Automatically to Google Sheets
                        </h1>
                        <p>
                          In this article, we will give you an example on how to
                          export receipts automatically from the Back Office to
                          Google sheets, using
                          <a
                            href="https://loyverse.com/marketplace/integromat"
                            rel="noopener external nofollow"
                            target="_blank"
                            >Make</a
                          >.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          Generally, it is only needed to create an account on
                          <strong>Make</strong> and the modules/tools that you
                          need for this scenario are the following:
                          <strong
                            >Flow Control: Iterator, Tools: Text Aggregator, Get
                            Store, Get POS, Google Sheets: Create a Spreadsheet,
                            Google Sheets: Add a row</strong
                          >. Next, simply go to the Make website, sign in after
                          creating an account and click on the
                          <strong>Create a New Scenario</strong> button and then
                          follow the steps provided down below.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image24.png"
                            alt="Create a Spreadsheet, Google Sheets"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          <strong
                            >How to connect Loyverse to Make: Note that this is
                            a general process on connecting an account of other
                            services to Make, so the steps to add Google Sheet
                            or Loyverse will be the same as provided
                            here.</strong
                          >
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          1. Click on the Connect App button from the
                          <a href="https://loyverse.com/marketplace"
                            >Loyverse App Marketplace</a
                          >
                          website.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image13.png"
                            alt="Connect App button from the Loyverse App Marketplace"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          2. It will redirect you to the Make webpage to log in
                          to your account and then will direct you to their
                          website where you can create this scenario.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          3. From the scenario page, click on the “+” button and
                          select the List Receipts Module.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image12.png"
                            alt="select the List Receipts Module"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          4. Next, Click on the dropdown bar button that says
                          <strong>Connection Type</strong>. This menu will allow
                          you to select and set up the account to connect to
                          Make from Loyverse (or Google Sheet since they are the
                          same process). Select the “Loyverse OAuth2.0” and then
                          add its name and then click on save. Once you have
                          clicked on save, it will then open another page where
                          you will have to log in your user credentials for
                          Loyverse and click on the <strong>Allow</strong>. The
                          process is the same with Google Sheets as well.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image15.png"
                            alt="Settings"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image27.png"
                            alt="Filter date"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          5. Next add the
                          <strong
                            >Google Sheets Module for Create a
                            Spreadsheet</strong
                          >. You have to connect your Google account onto the
                          <strong>Make</strong> first by clicking on the Add
                          button, name the connection and then click on save.
                          After clicking on the save button, another window will
                          appear that leads you to Google to allow access for
                          the information to Make.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image1.png"
                            alt="appear that leads you to Google to allow access for the information to Make"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          6. Fill up the field for Title of the Spreadsheet and
                          as well the title field for spreadsheet. You can
                          follow the naming made in the screenshots.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image17.png"
                            alt="ill up the field for Title of the Spreadsheet"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          a) The following information in the screenshot
                          provided above can be found in the pop up window that
                          will appear once you click on the field. To do this,
                          click on the Calendar Icon and then select the
                          <em>formatDate and now</em> then type the date format
                          that you’d like for them to appear:
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image20.png"
                            alt="Calendar Icon and then select the formatDate"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          7. Since we have established connection, we can now
                          set a filter for the List Receipts module so that our
                          scenario knows what data needs to get in or not. Click
                          on the dotted line or link and a pop up window will
                          appear that has the field of condition.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image5.png"
                            alt="pop up window will appear that has the field of condition"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          8. From the Condition Field, fill it up with the
                          information of the receipt date that came from the
                          <strong>List Receipt</strong> and the Calendar. Use
                          the format date function to change the format of the
                          date to the format of
                          <strong>DD/MM/YYYY</strong> (note: this can be in any
                          format that you desire but please make the format the
                          same as possible on all the fields.).
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image18.png"
                            alt="change the format of the date"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          9.To find and fill the condition field of
                          <em>formatDate</em>, <em>receipt_date</em>, and
                          <em>now</em> like in the picture provided, you will
                          have to click on the field and another pop up window
                          appear. Just like in the screenshot, you are able to
                          fill out the field and add the <em>formatDate</em>,
                          <em>receipt_date</em> and <em>now</em> under the
                          Calendar Icon, click on the Star Icon and from there,
                          you can find the <em>receipt_date</em>.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image14.png"
                            alt="able to fill out the field"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          10. Next, add the
                          <strong>Google Sheets Module</strong> for Add a Row
                          after the <strong>Create SpreadSheet</strong> Module.
                          In this module, you will need to change the
                          <strong>Choose a Method</strong> field to
                          <strong>Enter Manually</strong>. The
                          <strong>Select from the list</strong> does not allow
                          you to add <strong>Spreadsheet ID</strong> that is why
                          you need to set it to <strong>Enter Manually</strong>.
                          Then in the Spreadsheet field, select the
                          <strong>Spreadsheet ID</strong> and fill the Sheet
                          Name the same as the screenshot. (Note: It is
                          important to be mindful in order to avoid any error,
                          the name of the Sheet entered here should match and be
                          the same with what you entered in step 6).
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image10.png"
                            alt="change the Choose a Method field"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          11. To find the <strong>Spreadsheet ID</strong>, click
                          on the field, click on the Star Icon and find the
                          Spreadsheet ID. And for the Sheet Name, you can go to
                          the Calendar Icon to do the same. Scroll down and fill
                          out the remaining fields in the following manner:
                          <strong
                            >Date, Receipt Number, Total Amount, Discount,
                            Taxes, Tips, Cost of Goods, Items, Dining Options,
                            POS, Stores</strong
                          >. Note: This is customizable depending on the
                          preference.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image2.png"
                            alt="go to the Calendar Icon"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image21.png"
                            alt="fill out the remaining fields in the following manner"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          12. Next, add the module
                          <strong>Flow Control: Iterator</strong>. After adding
                          and connecting the Module, open its menu and add the
                          information that has the name of “<strong
                            >Line Items[]” specifically</strong
                          >. And then click OK.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image28.png"
                            alt="open menu and add the information that has the name"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          13. Afterwards, add the module
                          <strong>Tools: Text Aggregator</strong>. This will
                          combine all the information of the Line Items into 1
                          cell. In its Source Module field, select
                          <strong>Iterator[n]</strong> to target the Iterator
                          module set up earlier, then in the Text Field, Click
                          on the field and select the
                          <strong>Item Name</strong> from the
                          <strong>Flow Control Module</strong> like in the
                          screenshot. And then Click OK.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image16.png"
                            alt="combine all the information of the Line Items into 1 cell"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          14. Next, add the <strong>Get a Store</strong> Module
                          to get the name of the store from Loyverse instead of
                          some random words. In the Store ID field, look for the
                          <strong>List Of Receipts</strong>. From there, find
                          the <strong>Store ID</strong> and place it into the
                          field. And then click OK.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image19.png"
                            alt="find the Store ID and place it into the field"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          15. Next, add the <strong>Get a POS</strong> module,
                          this will allow you to be able to get the name of the
                          POS. In the same manner on how to find the Store ID to
                          put in the field, you can find the
                          <strong>POS Device ID</strong> and place it in the
                          <strong>POS Device ID field</strong>. And then click
                          on OK.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image11.png"
                            alt="find the POS Device ID and place it in the POS Device ID field"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          16. Then, lastly, you can add the
                          <strong>Add a Row</strong> Module again in order to
                          send receipts in the Google Sheet. Again, set the
                          <strong>Choose a Method Field</strong> to
                          <strong>Enter Manually</strong>, Fill the
                          <strong>Spreadsheet Field</strong> with the
                          <strong>Spreadsheet ID</strong>, from the
                          <strong
                            >Google Sheets: Create a Spreadsheet Module</strong
                          >
                          in the Star Icon, then fill out the Sheet Name field
                          exactly with the previous google spreadsheet modules.
                          Note: The Spreadsheet Name should be exactly the same
                          as what you have entered in the previous Add a row
                          Module. Otherwise, an error will occur.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image3.png"
                            alt="add the Add a Row Module again in order to send receipts in the Google Sheet"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          17. Then, scroll down the window to fill out the
                          following fields of rows as follows in the screenshot
                          provided. <strong>Note:</strong> In the receipt_date,
                          the date it represents is not formatted to the date
                          that you are expecting. If you wish to format it to
                          your actual date that you are expecting then you can
                          encase the <strong>receipt_date</strong> with the
                          funcion:
                          <strong
                            >formatDate(receipt_date;DD/MM/YYYY HH;mm;
                            timezone*)</strong
                          >
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image.png"
                            alt="receipt_date with the funcion: formatDate"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image23.png"
                            alt="scroll down the window to fill out the following fields of rows as follows in the screenshot provided"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image8.png"
                            alt="scroll down the window to fill out the following fields of rows as follows in the screenshot provided"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image25.png"
                            alt="scroll down the window to fill out the following fields of rows as follows in the screenshot provided"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image22.png"
                            alt="scroll down the window to fill out the following fields of rows as follows in the screenshot provided"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          18. If you’d like to also catch errors when the
                          scenario returns an error in the
                          <strong>Get a POS Module</strong>, right click the
                          bubble of the Get a POS module and click on the
                          <strong>Add an Error Handler</strong> button. The make
                          will deviate and create a new path for you to do the
                          following:
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image4.png"
                            alt="The make will deviate and create a new path"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          a) Creating and adding another
                          <strong>Add a Row</strong> module that is created
                          exactly the same way with step 11 to 12.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image9.png"
                            alt="Creating and adding another Add a Row module"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          b) Except that in the last part, remove the POS Name
                          or do not add it and leave it blank. That way, there
                          will be no more errors.
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image7.png"
                            alt="remove the POS Name or do not add it and leave it blank"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          19. Then, you can use Make’s features to make the work
                          of this scenario automated. This is entirely up to the
                          user’s preference so in this example, the scenario for
                          this is to run this everyday at 7:00 pm:
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image26.png"
                            alt="use Make’s features to make the work of this scenario automated"
                            width="800"
                            height="600"
                          />
                        </p>
                        <p>&nbsp;</p>
                        <p>
                          20. Run the scenario once to check if everything is in
                          order and no error appears and this is what it should
                          look like as a result:
                        </p>
                        <p>
                          <img
                            class="img-in-blog"
                            src="/sites/all/themes/loyversecom/images/blog/31-image6.png"
